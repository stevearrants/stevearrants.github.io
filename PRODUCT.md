# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

A mix of engineering leaders, CTOs, startup founders, PMs, and technical writer peers. All are evaluating Steve's expertise and deciding whether to hire him or recommend him. They arrive skeptical: the market is full of generalist writers claiming specialist skills. They're looking for signals of genuine depth — not credentials, but evidence of systems thinking, technical range, and professional rigor.

Context: they're usually comparing against other consultants or internal hires. A few minutes of reading, not days of research.

## Product Purpose

Green Mountain Docs is Steve Arrants' independent technical writing consultancy. The site's job is to convert credible interest into a contact. It does that by demonstrating — not claiming — expertise: through the specificity of his case studies, the clarity of his service framing, and the quality of the writing and design itself. A documentation consultant's own site is a portfolio piece. If it reads like a generic freelancer template, the implied message undermines the pitch.

## Brand Personality

Expert · Precise · Direct

Voice: senior advisor. No hedging. No marketing softener language. Short declarative sentences. The kind of person who answers "what's wrong with your docs?" in one paragraph, not a capabilities deck. Confidence comes from depth, not from enthusiasm.

## Anti-references

- Generic freelancer portfolio templates (Upwork profiles, Wix/Squarespace starter pages, "hire me" sites with soft-focus hero photos and bullet-point skill lists)
- Startup/SaaS marketing aesthetic: gradient blobs, hero metrics, glassmorphism, feature-grid cards with icons above every row

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The site's own information architecture should demonstrate the same rigor Steve brings to client documentation. Findable, scannable, no dead ends.
2. **Show, don't claim.** Specificity over superlatives. Case study numbers and concrete outcomes carry more weight than adjectives like "proven" or "expert."
3. **Signal-to-noise.** Every design element earns its place. Decoration that doesn't carry meaning should be cut; it's the same standard Steve applies to documentation prose.
4. **Expert confidence.** The site speaks to people who can tell the difference. Don't explain obvious things. Don't hedge. Don't over-persuade.
5. **The site is a sample.** Visitors are hiring a writer. The copy, the hierarchy, and the structure are themselves a writing sample. They judge accordingly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 against background. Large/bold text ≥ 3:1. All interactive elements keyboard-navigable. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. No reliance on color alone for meaning.
